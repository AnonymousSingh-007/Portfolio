My work sits at the intersection of AI security, multi-agent systems, and applied cryptography — three domains that turn out to share a single underlying question: what is the structural condition that makes a category of system exploitable, not just the one weak instance of it.

That question produced a taxonomy of LLM attack surfaces (rather than a single exploit), an architectural critique of quantum key distribution's trust model, and an ongoing study of emergent coordination failure in multi-agent reinforcement learning systems used for drone swarm jamming-avoidance.

I compete in CTFs because the instincts transfer, but the CTF results are supporting evidence of execution ability — not the headline.
