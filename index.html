I work at the intersection of AI security, multi-agent systems, and applied cryptography — three domains sharing one structural question: what makes an entire category of system exploitable, not just a single weak instance?

That question produced a taxonomy of LLM adversarial surfaces (Best Paper, ICEM 2024), an ongoing MARL study of emergent coordination failure in drone swarms at DIAT, and a behavioral-biometric adversarial primitive (MIMIC). CTF results are execution evidence — not the headline.

Prompt Injection & Jailbreaking — a comprehensive taxonomy of adversarial attacks on LLMs.

NCTAAI 4.0 · 2024

A structural taxonomy of LLM adversarial attack surfaces — twelve categories across injection, jailbreaking, evasion, and extraction — designed as a map of failure conditions rather than a single exploit chain.

Multi-Agent RL for drone swarm jamming avoidance.

DIAT (DRDO-affiliated) · target: IEEE TNNLS

A QMIX-based multi-agent architecture for coordinated jammer avoidance. The work identifies emergent policy decoupling through behavioral observation and traces it to mechanism — addressed via a KL-anchored Navigator. Pre-publication.

QMIXcooperative MARL jammer avoidancepolicy decoupling KL anchoring

MIMIC — hybrid LSTM/Diffusion synthesis of human mouse motion.

Originally: IEEE RA-L (desk-rejected, scope mismatch) · Redirecting: IEEE TIFS

An LSTM/DDPM hybrid synthesizing realistic human mouse-movement trajectories — studied as a behavioral biometric adversarial primitive, exploring bot-detection evasion and the limits of motion-based authentication.
